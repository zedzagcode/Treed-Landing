Tree’d is a three-part audio-guide ecosystem: the Handset, the Tree, and the Dashboard. Combined, they create a multilingual, AI-powered visitor experience that’s simple to deploy and effortless to run, yet game-changing for museum visitors.

## **What Tree’d is made up of**

![Close-up of red and black telephone handset speakers facing each other.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/691b0e7ef8c00707a6e7d175/5aa9285e-32da-4e40-8ad2-36ce995caf2c/Side-to-side+close+up+3-2+glossy.png)

### The Handset

The Handset is the visitor’s companion: a screen-free, vintage-inspired audio device that lets visitors tap exhibits, listen to museum-approved stories, and ask follow-up questions in real time. It supports over a dozen languages and runs up to 6 hours on a single charge.

[Learn More](https://www.treed.co/the-handset)

![A modern, sculptural lamp with a bronze finish and black accents, standing on a circular white pedestal in a grand, classical-style interior with stone stairs and arches.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/691b0e7ef8c00707a6e7d175/10f44ba3-d45f-4c9c-ac4d-345167c132ed/Full.png)

### The Tree

The Tree is the system’s central hub. It keeps every handset charged, synced, and automatically updated with no technical effort. It also distributes content and AI knowledge to the handsets, presents them cleanly for visitor pickup, and provides efficient, high-density storage.

[Learn More](https://www.treed.co/overview)

### The Dashboard

The Dashboard gives museums real-time visibility into visitor engagement, language mix, and popular artifacts. It provide a range of key metrics that help museums make informed staffing decisions and understand the interests of different visitor groups.
