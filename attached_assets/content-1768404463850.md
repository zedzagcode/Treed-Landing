## **Simple steps, no learning curve.**

Once a visitor collects The Handset from the museum’s point of sale, no setup is required. A tap plays an audio story. A question sparks conversations. A seamless system keeps the focus exactly where it belongs: on the museum.

![Close-up of a language selection touchscreen with flags of the United Kingdom, Italy, Germany, and the Netherlands, and a red mouse on the screen.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/691b0e7ef8c00707a6e7d175/3de4b37d-30fe-4390-8b07-61d132d782ed/NFC+Language+Panel.png)

**Step 1: Select your language**

Start your journey by tapping your handset on the NFC Language Panel to choose the language for the tour. It is usually located near the museum’s ticket office.

![A marble statue of a man holding a red telephone receiver up to his ear, facing a glass display case with another marble bust of a man; the background is plain gray.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/691b0e7ef8c00707a6e7d175/5fccc26e-1405-4d50-b082-053cda5be994/NFC+Scan.png)

**Step 2: Choose an exhibit**

Exhibits have an NFC chip nearby. Just tap it with the Handset, and you’ll instantly hear the story behind the piece in around 60 seconds through a prerecorded audio track,

![A grayscale sculpture of a woman holding a bright red telephone receiver to her ear.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/691b0e7ef8c00707a6e7d175/e4f2ea2b-1ecb-41d1-951c-52041c796983/Statue+AI+Talking.png)

**Step 3: Start a conversation**

Have a question? Just ask. The handset listens, understands what you’re asking, and responds. All information provided by the AI model is museum-approved, meaning zero risk of misinformation.
